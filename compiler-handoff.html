Recommended Work Sequence

  1. Open the chronology with the default public/declassified filter and read it straight through by year before selecting any chapter subset.
  2. Use Copy Notes or Export Notes before a selection meeting so every visible record has date, title, source note, source URL, PDF URL when available, chapter, and topics.
  3. Filter by chapter only after the eight-year chronology is in view. This protects the National Security Strategy chapter from being split into disconnected annual fragments.
  4. Treat PDD/PRD rows as directive handles until an underlying document, meeting record, draft, or decision memorandum is located.
  5. Use the Clinton Library Visit Planner to pull folders that answer known decision-path questions first, then use public statements to frame rather than substitute for internal records.

Compiler Risk Controls

  • The official FRUS volume status remains planned, not published.
  • This workspace is a source-control and triage aid, not a proposed final document selection.
  • Collection page counts are source coverage, not document page counts.
  • For Clinton Library folders, capture folder front, withdrawal sheet, exact title, date span, and routing marks before deep reading.
  • Separate public speech material from documents that disclose internal debate, decision, or implementation.
  • Treat AI-assisted ordering or summary as a checklist only; verify against the cited record before selection.
